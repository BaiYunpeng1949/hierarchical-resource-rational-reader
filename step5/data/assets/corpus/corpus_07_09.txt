The heart pumps blood through the body, supplying oxygen and nutrients.
The Amazon Rainforest, known as the lungs of the Earth, produces about twenty percent of the world's oxygen supply.
The Moon orbits Earth at an average distance of about four hundred thousand kilometers. Its gravitational pull causes ocean tides, influencing various marine and coastal ecosystems around the planet.
The Internet originated from ARPANET, a project funded by the American Department of Defense in the late nineteen sixties. It has since evolved into a global network connecting billions of devices, enabling unprecedented access to information and communication worldwide.
The human body is composed of approximately sixty percent water. This vital fluid is essential for numerous bodily functions, including regulating temperature, transporting nutrients, and removing waste. On average, an adult needs to drink about two liters of water daily to maintain proper hydration levels.
